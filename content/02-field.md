The field is a green or charcoal carpet with matte black walls, two coloured goals, and white boundary lines. Robots play on the carpet inside the white lines — the area between the lines and the walls is the "out area". Fields may be on a table or on the floor.

### Ball out of play

The rules for when the ball is "out" differ between Simple Simon / Standard and the higher leagues:

| League | Ball is out of play when... |
|---|---|
| **Simple Simon** | The ball strikes the wall behind either goal. If inclines are not used, the ball strikes any wall |
| **Standard** | If inclines are **not** used: the ball strikes any wall (including sides of goals). If inclines **are** used: the ball strikes the back wall or sides of goals |

> **What are inclines?** The out areas of the field may be raised by 10 mm on the outside edge, creating a gentle slope that rolls the ball back toward the playing area. Whether inclines are used affects ball-out-of-play rules.

### Key field dimensions

| Dimension | Value |
|---|---|
| Goal width (internal) | 450 mm |
| Goal depth (internal) | 74 mm |
| Crossbar height | 140 mm |
| White line thickness | 50 mm |
| White line distance from wall | 250 mm |
| Wall height (minimum) | 100 mm (220 mm at Nationals) |
| Dimension tolerance | 5% |

> **Design tip:** Robots should be built to cope with the 5% tolerance in field dimensions. Don't rely on exact measurements — test on different surfaces and field sizes when possible.
