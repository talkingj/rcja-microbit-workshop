points:
  - title: Autonomous only
    body: No remote control of any kind. Robots run downloaded programs and must be started manually by the team captain. They cannot be started from a laptop, tablet, or phone.
  - title: 2 x 5-minute halves
    body: Five-minute break between halves. The game clock runs continuously except during a referee's timeout. Some competitions may use 10-minute halves.
  - title: Kick-off
    body: Coin toss decides who kicks off or chooses ends. Ball placed at centre, non-kicking team's robots must have part of the robot in their penalty box. All robots start on the referee's command.
  - title: Scoring
    body: A goal is scored when the ball strikes the back wall of the goal. Own goals count for the opposition. After a goal, the non-scoring team gets the kick-off.
  - title: Lack of progress
    body: Called when the ball is stuck, no robot can reach it, or a robot is "forcing" the ball through a defender. Ball moves to the nearest neutral point (then centre if it happens again).
  - title: Damaged robots
    body: A robot is "damaged" if it doesn't respond to the ball, gets stuck for 20+ seconds, tips over, damages the ball or field, or makes noise from a speaker. Off the field for at least 30 seconds.
---

These are the key rules for Simple Simon and Standard League. Both leagues follow the same game rules — the differences are only in robot construction. For the complete rules, use the [RCJA Rules Reference Tool](https://talkingj.github.io/rcja-rules/).

  cap: Must move forward

  cap: One defender in the box
  desc: Only one robot from the defending team should be in the penalty area. If two defenders are in the box and affecting play, the less influential one is moved to the centre.

  cap: Attacking without the ball
  desc: If a robot continuously attacks or charges a robot that doesn't have the ball, the referee calls a foul. The fouling robot is deemed damaged. Repeated fouls lead to disqualification.

### Other important rules

- **10-goal mercy rule:** A game ends when there is a 10-goal difference. The losing team may elect to continue, but the score won't change.
- **Late penalty:** Teams lose one goal per minute if late to a match. After 10 minutes, the game is forfeited 10–0.
- **No free kicks, penalty kicks, or offside** — the game is simpler than real football.
- **Communication:** Robots can communicate wirelessly, but it must not interfere with other robots. Use unique message prefixes. The referee can ask you to disable communication.
- **Humans:** Only the team captain can handle robots during the game, and only at the referee's instruction. Other team members must stay more than 1 metre from the field.

> **The referee's decisions are final.** Arguing with a referee results in a yellow warning card. Continued argument means a red card and immediate forfeit. If you disagree with a call, note it calmly — protests can only be about scoring errors, and only before signing the score sheet.
