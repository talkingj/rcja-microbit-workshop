Knowing the rules and having a good robot is only half the battle. Being prepared for competition day — scrutineering, calibration, interviews, and match logistics — makes a big difference to how your team performs.

<!-- ACTIVITY_CARDS
- title: Scrutineering
  cap: First thing each morning
  desc: Robots are checked for size (220 mm cylinder), weight (1 kg max), ball capture zone (30 mm), and construction legality. Happens every morning. If you modify your robot during the day, you must get re-inspected.
- title: Calibration
  cap: Before each game
  desc: At least 5 minutes of setup time before each match. Use this to calibrate IR sensors and colour sensors for the venue's lighting and magnetic conditions. Every venue is different.
- title: Team interview
  cap: During the competition
  desc: A panel will interview your team about your design, programming, and process. You must demonstrate that the robot is your own work and that every team member understands the code.
- title: Round robin
  cap: Group stage
  desc: 3 points for a win, 1 for a draw, 0 for a loss. Teams ranked by total points, then goals scored, then goal difference. Play fair and learn from every match.
- title: Finals
  cap: Top teams
  desc: Play-offs for 1st through 4th. Tied finals use "golden goal" — play continues until someone scores. After 5 extra minutes with no goal, only one robot per team is allowed on the field.
-->

### Preparation checklist

<div class="pitch-grid">
  <div class="pitch-card">
    <div class="pitch-num">01</div>
    <div class="pitch-title">Annotated code</div>
    <div class="pitch-body">Submit before the competition. Your code must have comments explaining sensor use, decision-making logic, and motor control. You can keep updating after submission, but maintain an annotated version.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="pitch-card">
    <div class="pitch-num">02</div>
    <div class="pitch-title">Digital poster</div>
    <div class="pitch-body">A3 size. Must include: team name, member first names and roles, one innovative feature, one challenge you overcame, and what you learned. Required for special awards. No personal info beyond first names.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="pitch-card">
    <div class="pitch-num">03</div>
    <div class="pitch-title">Spare parts</div>
    <div class="pitch-body">Bring extra LEGO pieces, cables, and a charger. You can repair between matches, but modifications must be re-inspected. Don't get caught without a critical part.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="pitch-card">
    <div class="pitch-num">04</div>
    <div class="pitch-title">Practice on carpet</div>
    <div class="pitch-body">Sensors behave differently on different surfaces. If possible, practice on carpet similar to competition carpet (3–5 mm thick, green or charcoal). The Gen2 vinyl mat works for practice.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="pitch-card">
    <div class="pitch-num">05</div>
    <div class="pitch-title">Know your roles</div>
    <div class="pitch-body">Designate a captain — they handle robots during the game. Other members start one robot each, then must stay 1+ metre from the field. Cover any cyan, yellow, or orange on your clothing if asked.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="pitch-card">
    <div class="pitch-num">06</div>
    <div class="pitch-title">Spirit of competition</div>
    <div class="pitch-body">Share what you've learned with other teams. Ask questions. Be respectful to referees — their decisions are final. The goal is learning, not just winning.</div>
  </div>
</div>

> **Late penalty:** Teams lose one goal per minute if late to a match. After 10 minutes, the game is forfeited 10–0. The 10 minutes includes any break time — be ready early.

> **After the game:** If both captains are happy with the result, they sign the score sheet. After signing, no protests can be lodged — so check the score before you sign.
